Getting started with your jurney:

  • 1. Start with a short, simple program.
  • 2. Choose the right amount of weight to lift
  • 3. Warm up first.
  • 4. Focus on having a good form to avoid injuries and effectiveness.
  • 5. Give yourself at least a day of rest to recover.
  • 6. Aim to challenge yourself, not overtax yourself.
  • 7. Change things up after 6 or more weeks of consistent training.

Sets, Reps, and Weigtht

  • To lose body fat and build muscle:

    • Use enough weight that it's challenging to complete 8 to 12 repetitions and 1 to 3 sets—1 for beginners, 2 to 3 for intermediate and advanced exercisers. Rest about 30 seconds to 1 minute between sets and at least one day between workout sessions.

  • For muscle gain:

    • Use enough weight that you can only complete 4 to 8 repetitions and 3 or more sets, resting for 1 to 2 minutes between sets and 2 to 3 days between sessions. For beginners, give yourself several weeks of conditioning before you tackle weight training with this degree of difficulty. You may need a spotter for many exercises.

  • For health and muscular endurance:

    • Use enough weight that you can only complete 12 to 16 repetitions, 1 to 3 sets, resting 20 to 30 seconds between sets, and at least one day between workout sessions.

Diet Building Muscle

Macros are the three types of nutrients that you need the most in your diet: carbohydrate, fat, and protein.

  • Carbohydrates are sugars, grains, starches, and fiber.
  • Fats are in oils, dairy, meat, nuts, and seeds. Vegetables have small amounts of fat.
  • Protein is mostly found in meats, poultry, eggs, dairy, and fish, and in smaller amounts in legumes, nuts, seeds, and vegetables.

Proteins are essential for muscle growth and many other functions in the body.

All About Counting Macros

Counting macros means keeping track of the amount and ratio of macronutrients in your diet. You may count the total number of grams of each macro or the percentage of your daily calories.
